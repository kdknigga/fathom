Investment Return Rate

The annual return you could earn by investing your money instead of paying upfront. For example, if you could earn 7% in a stock index fund, that's the opportunity cost of paying cash.

Inflation reduces the real value of future payments. At 3% inflation, a $100 payment next year only costs you about $97 in today's dollars. Higher inflation makes longer loan terms slightly cheaper in real terms.

Your marginal tax rate is the rate on your last dollar of income -- not your average rate. If your loan interest is tax-deductible, this rate determines how much you save. A 24% marginal rate means each $1,000 in deductible interest saves $240 in taxes.
